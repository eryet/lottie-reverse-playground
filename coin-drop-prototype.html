Motion Prototype

Coin drop with decaying flips and a final settle.

This first-pass canvas prototype follows a simple three-phase motion model: vertical drop, energy-losing floor interaction, and a tiny final wobble. The implementation keeps timing and motion constants explicit so it stays easy to tune in later review rounds.

Phase: Drop

Primary tuning constants

Drop speed

Adjust DROP_END_Y, DROP_DURATION, DROP_SQUASH, and DROP_START_X to control the initial descent, impact timing, lateral entry, and contact compression.

Bounce and settle

Adjust BOUNCE_HEIGHT, BOUNCE_DURATION, SETTLE_DURATION, and ROLL_TRAVEL to change how much energy remains after the first hit, how far the coin travels, and how long it takes to rest.

Flip decay

Adjust INITIAL_ROTATIONS, DECAY_ROTATIONS, WOBBLE_ANGLE, MIN_FACE_WIDTH, and PERSPECTIVE_LIFT to control spin count, energy loss, side-view readability, depth offset, and final wobble amplitude.
